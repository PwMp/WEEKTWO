Animated Chess Board - Designed by Madhumali

 

https://a360.co/3s1gwXx

 

 

Fusion 360 is a powerful Computer-Aided Design (CAD) and Computer-Aided Engineering (CAE) software that allows you to simulate a wide range of mechanisms and physical behaviors.

 

In our previous design we discussed about how to simulate a mechanism. With this design, we are going to discuss about prototyping 3D models and animating them using Fusion 360.

 

 

Step 1: Import one vector diagram of chess pieces to the fusion workplace to use it as a guide to sketch the chess pieces.

Step 2: Draw the basic sketch following the diagram and then using the command revolve, you can get the solid model.

 

Step 3: You can complete chess pieces by following the same stepts. Once done save them all as components as we will need the in the final design.

 

Step 4: Draw the base of the chess board. You can follow the technical drawing below.

Step 5: Draw one 40*40 mm square and place it in one corner of the base board. You can then use the Rectangular pattern option to repeat the remaining squares.

Step 6: Now make this board together as component and place it to the ground. In this way when we are moving the chess pieces the board will not be moved.

 

Step 7: Insert previously made components for chess pieces and place them on chess board as required. When one side is done, you can use the mirror option to easily place the pieces on the other side.

Now you have your chess board ready!

 

 

Step 8: From the design menu bar change your workspace to animation. Then by selecting transformation option you can replace your components as you wish and can get a cool animation of your work.
